Using the lab signup command!

Here is an example usage of the command:

We start by simply running cs033_lab_signups:

$ cs033_lab_signups

We are greeted with the following output:

Welcome to cs33! Please list (space-separated) all the labs you can make from the following list:
Lab 1: Monday 4-6
Lab 2: Monday 6-8
Lab 3: Monday 8-10
Lab 4: Monday 10-12
Lab 5: Tuesday 7-9
Lab 6: Tuesday 9-11

We then type our labs. This can be in a variety of formats. For the sake of simplicity, let's just list the lab numbers. I see that I can make any lab on monday, so I type

1 2 3 4

and then press enter. The output on my screen now looks like this:

Welcome to cs33! Please list (space-separated) all the labs you can make from the following list:
Lab 1: Monday 4-6
Lab 2: Monday 6-8
Lab 3: Monday 8-10
Lab 4: Monday 10-12
Lab 5: Tuesday 7-9
Lab 6: Tuesday 9-11
1 2 3 4
signing up for Lab 1: Monday 4-6
signing up for Lab 2: Monday 6-8
signing up for Lab 3: Monday 8-10
signing up for Lab 4: Monday 10-12


If you have any concerns about your lab signups or this signup program has malfunctioned, please email cs033tas@cs.brown.edu

And you're done! You are only signed up for a lab section if you see a confirmation message. These are the messages like 'signing up for Lab x: Xday x-x' That print out after you have listed your availability.

Remember, lab signups are not first-come first-served. We will attempt to schedule you optimially.

You will have a lab time assigned and emailed to you by Sunday night.
